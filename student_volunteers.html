    Acceptance Notification

Student volunteers play a crucial role in ensuring the success of ICSME. They help with everything from registration to local support. Being a student volunteer is an excellent opportunity to meet, interact, and network with leading academic and industrial researchers and practitioners in the area of Software Engineering. Such interactions often help shape the research direction for many a student. Being a student volunteer is also a great way to meet other students from all over the world that share your interest in Software Engineering as well as reduce some of the costs of the conference!

This exclusive experience is available to undergraduate and graduate students from all over the world who are enthusiastic and willing to contribute to make ICSME 2019 a unique experience for all attendees.

Would you like to be part of the ICSME 2019 organization? Don’t miss your chance! Sign up and help us!

Responsibilities

Approximately 20 hours of work during the conference week:

  • Registration duties (e.g., setting up, preparing conference bags, and helping attendees register).
  • Technical assistance to speakers and attendees.
  • Providing directions to various locations at the conference location.
  • Checking badges at various halls including dining halls.
  • Other similar tasks.
  • Availability from September 30 – Oct 04, 2019 (inclusive). You must specify in the application form if you are not available on any of the days of the pre- and/or post-conference program. Not all volunteers will be required for the full duration of the conference, so we will take this information into account during scheduling.

Benefits

  • Complimentary student registration to the main conference, as well as the ability to attend the sessions that you have been assigned to.
  • Networking opportunities with international software engineering researchers, practitioners, and fellow students.
  • Coffee breaks, lunches and conference banquet tickets.
  • Conference T-Shirt.

Please note that travel and accommodation costs are not included. We encourage all students to apply for financial assistance through the SIGSOFT CAPS program at http://www.sigsoft.org/resources/caps.html.

Qualifications

  • Must be enrolled as a doctoral, masters, or full-time undergraduate student at the time of the conference.
  • Must be proficient in English and have good social and communication skills.

Application and Procedure

  • Complete the online application form at https://forms.gle/gzWjswxKtxdWhVqXA
  • Arrange for a letter of reference from your academic supervisor or other faculty members at your institution. This recommendation must be emailed to the Student Volunteer Chair (cdnvse@rit.edu) with the subject line “ICSME2019SV Reference for <Student Name>”) by the faculty reference you indicate in the application form. The letter should cover your organizational skills, willingness to help, proficiency in English, and anything else that will help us to select you as a student volunteer.

Visa and Funding Options

  • Visa Support Letters: If you need a visa support letter to apply for a visa, please visit the Visa Information page.
  • We also suggest checking for travel support from national research funding agencies from your country, e.g., a limited number of travel stipends for US-based minorities and women may be provided through an NSF Travel Award Program.
  • If your university requires specific information not included in the Notification of Selection as a Student Volunteer in order to approve funding for travel, be sure to let the Student Volunteers Co-Chairs know as soon as possible.

A note to student volunteer applicants with an accepted paper at ICSME

The notification for the student volunteer track arrives after the registration deadline for accepted papers in several ICSME tracks. Students who are selected as volunteers will be reimbursed for registration fees if they needed to register before the early bird deadline.

Availability

Student volunteers must be available from September 30 – Oct 04, 2019 (inclusive).

Please note that you will be requested to specify in the application form if you are not available on any of the days of the pre and/or post-conference program. Not all volunteers will be required at all times, and we can take this information into account during selection.
